How I work

My PM approach

I don't believe in one-size-fits-all frameworks. But there are a few principles I bring to every team and product.

🎯

Ruthless prioritization

If everything is a priority, nothing is. I help teams make hard calls with clear criteria — and I'm comfortable being the one to say no.

🔍

Discovery before solutions

I run structured user research to surface surprises, not confirmations. The best product decisions come from what users do, not what they say they'd buy.

📐

Systems thinking

My supply chain background means I see products as systems — with upstream dependencies, feedback loops, and failure modes most PMs miss.

📝

Writing that creates alignment

My PRDs don't need a meeting to explain them. I write to create shared understanding — not to document decisions after they've already been made.

📊

Outcome-based measurement

I measure success by what changes for the user, not by what shipped. Every initiative gets a clear outcome statement before it starts.

🤝

Influence without authority

PMs have no direct reports but are accountable for everything. I build trust through consistency and adapt my framing to what each stakeholder actually cares about.

How I think

A few things I believe about product

  • 01.

    Shipping is a hypothesis, not a conclusion.

    Launching is when the learning starts. I treat every release as an experiment and keep a tight feedback loop to know whether we got it right.

  • 02.

    The best product decisions are also good writing.

    If you can't explain a tradeoff clearly in a doc, you probably don't understand it well enough to make it. Writing forces clarity.

  • 03.

    Constraints are a feature, not a bug.

    Supply chain taught me that limited time, money, and capacity force creative prioritization. I bring that discipline to software.

  • 04.

    Care about the problem, not your solution to it.

    The most dangerous PM in any room is one who's fallen in love with their roadmap. I stay attached to the user's outcome, not my answer.
